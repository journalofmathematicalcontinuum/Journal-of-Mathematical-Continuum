Journal of Mathematical Continuum

Aims and Scope

Aims

The Journal of Mathematical Continuum aims to advance high quality research across the interconnected domains of mathematical sciences. The journal is founded on the principle that mathematics represents a unified continuum of ideas, evolving from fundamental theoretical structures to applied and computational innovations. It seeks to promote rigorous reasoning, conceptual clarity, analytical depth, and methodological originality in both classical and emerging areas of mathematics.

The journal aspires to provide an international scholarly platform that encourages intellectual integration between pure mathematical theory and its broad spectrum of applications in science, engineering, economics, technology, and decision sciences.

Scope

The journal welcomes original research articles, review papers, and significant theoretical contributions in areas including, but not limited to:

  • Pure Mathematics and Foundational Theory
  • Functional and Structural Analysis
  • Algebraic and Geometric Structures
  • Probability Theory and Stochastic Processes
  • Optimization Theory and Mathematical Programming
  • Dynamical Systems and Differential Equations
  • Mathematical Modelling and Applied Analysis
  • Computational and Numerical Mathematics
  • Interdisciplinary Quantitative Methods

Submissions must demonstrate originality, mathematical rigor, logical consistency, and scholarly significance. Interdisciplinary studies are particularly encouraged when they preserve mathematical depth while addressing real world analytical challenges.
